### Keeping Dogs Cool in Summer

1. **Never leave in a car** — Even with windows cracked, cars reach 100°F+ within 10 minutes.
2. **Know the signs** — Excessive panting, drooling, lethargy, red gums, vomiting are signs of heatstroke.
3. **Cooling mat ≠ AC** — Mats cool by absorption/evaporation, not refrigeration. They help but don't replace climate control.
4. **Gel vs water vs evaporative** — Gel is no-fuss. Water lasts longer. Evaporative works best in dry climates.

## FAQ

**Are cooling mats safe for dogs?**

Yes — most use non-toxic gel or water. Check for chew-resistance if your dog is a destructive chewer.

**Do cooling mats need refrigeration?**

No — most are pressure-activated. Refrigeration can make them too cold and damage the gel.
